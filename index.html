The UK fishing industry will need continued access to EU markets if it is to thrive after Brexit, a House of Lords report has warned. It also warns that Britain may have to allow EU-registered boats to fish in UK waters as part of an overall deal. Fishing regions around the UK voted heavily in favour of leaving the EU during the referendum campaign. The Lords review says these communities are at risk of being marginalised in the wider Brexit negotiations.

Nine people have died in Vancouver in the past 24 hours from an overdose of the painkiller fentanyl opioid, officials say. Mayor Gregor Robertson said it was "desperate times" for the Canadian city, adding that more deaths were expected. "It's hard to see any silver lining right now when we haven't hit rock bottom," Mr Robertson said. Drug abuse in Canada claimed the lives of 2,000 people in 2015.

David Hockney's home city of Bradford is to honour the artist by opening a permanent gallery dedicated to his work to mark his 80th birthday. The David Hockney Gallery will be housed in the city's Cartwright Hall. "I used to love going to Cartwright Hall as a kid," Hockney said in a statement. "It was the only place in Bradford I could see real paintings." The new gallery will show works ranging from early sketches to well-known paintings and his iPad drawings. It will open on 7 July - two days before his 80th birthday.
